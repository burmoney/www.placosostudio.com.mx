The Resilient Journey of Placoso Studio: From Darkness to Triumph

4 min read · Feb 26, 2026 · Mexico

My journey with Placoso Studio has been anything but easy. It’s a story of resilience, pain, setbacks, and ultimately, rebirth.

I started Placoso Studio as a service for custom clothing and accessories, specializing in high-quality printing and embroidery. I wanted to create products like shirts, hats, and hoodies to bring people’s designs to life.

Over time, I saw my project gaining traction — evolving from an idea, to a tattoo studio, to a clothing brand.

But then life threw me a curveball. I had to restart everything.

My girl left me, took my equipment, and I found myself in a deep depression. At the same time, I was dealing with my mother’s recovery from brain surgery — facing the possibility of losing her forever.

2024 was one of the hardest years of my life. I felt completely lost, with no support — just straight hustle.

I forgot who I was. Even though I’ve always been influential, I found myself seeking solitude, trying to escape the chaos.

This was my third attempt at launching my project — but this time, I needed more than success. I needed healing.

While dealing with work, family issues, and heartbreak, I watched everyone else move forward. I kept asking myself:

“When will I reach the next level?”

I was 33, feeling stagnant, unsure of my next move. I moved back home to care for my mother, who slowly recovered — from bedridden… to walking again.

After nearly three years, I found space to reconnect with my passion — art and music.

But just as I was preparing to relaunch… another setback hit.

All my accounts were hacked. Everything I built was stolen.

I had invested over $20,000 into systems, research, and growth — gone. It felt like someone was trying to erase me.

I hit rock bottom in September. Lost clients. Lost access. Lost direction.

But I didn’t quit.

My mom recovered. Life stabilized. But in December, I faced reality again — alone, broke, starting over.

I kept working. Saving. Building quietly. Supported by people who believed in me.

And slowly… everything began to change.

In 2026, I rebuilt. Stronger. Sharper. Unstoppable.

I remembered who I am — Burzache Milliones, owner of Placoso Studio.

I realized nothing was lost. Everything was part of the process.

The pain, the setbacks, the sacrifices — they built me.

Now my work is more powerful than ever.

And I share this to remind you:

No matter how dark it gets — resilience and faith will bring you back.

Thank you for supporting my journey.

This is just the beginning.
